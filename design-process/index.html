Design Process

#

In Notes on the Synthesis of Form, the design process is defined by the "program", a modular hierarchy of misfits. This program is created using a top-down approach but the solution is synthesized by assembling the solutions in a bottom-up approach.

#

In The Principles of Design, Nam Suh describes the design process using a top-down approach. The designer defines a minimal set of functional requirements for a given level. She then designs a solution to fulfill these requirements. This solution will then generate the requirements for the lower level. This back and forth process continues until the product is fully designed. The design process is then a "continuous interplay between what we want to achieve and how we want to achieve it."

#

In Design Rules, Baldwin and Clark shows that "the design structure of an artifact and the task structure of the design process are isomorphic."
